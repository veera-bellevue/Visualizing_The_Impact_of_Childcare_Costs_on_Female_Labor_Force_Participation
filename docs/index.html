Impact of Childcare Costs on Female Labor Force Participation

Overview

This project explores the relationship between childcare costs and female labor force participation in the United States, providing actionable insights for policymakers to address barriers to women's employment.

Key Features

  • State-level childcare costs analysis
  • Comparative insights on geographic disparities
  • Policy recommendations for improving access

Key Insights

  • Significant cost variation by state and region
  • Strong correlation between infant/toddler care costs and labor force participation
  • State-specific findings for California, West Virginia, and Idaho

Technologies Used

  • Python 3.x
  • Pandas, NumPy, Matplotlib, Seaborn
  • Jupyter Notebook
